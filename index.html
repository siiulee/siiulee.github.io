Imagine an app that encapsulates your cherished moments on the surface of the Moon, preserving them for eternity. Beyond the constraints of physical space, you can place a photograph of your chosen moment on the Moon, granting it a sense of immortality and storing it permanently in the Moon Garden. This design captures simple and human moments while also purifying and rejuvenating one's inner space. It merges the tangible and intangible into a unique and enduring blend of virtual and real spaces, serving as an alternative for those situations where viewing the Moon directly is difficult.

By capturing and eternally preserving these moments, the Moon Garden app redefines the essence of memory, allowing us to cherish significant life events across time and space.

DROP OFF

This project emphasises design as a tool for articulating our humanity. The design explores our collective dreams and struggles, seeking to understand our place in an unstable world through the expression of archetypes. Our cultural, social, political, and economic challenges ultimately aim at understanding humanity. This involves discovering the essential yet often overlooked human elements in our daily lives.

Inspired by a personal experience with the Moon at 3 AM, the project questions the lost spaces and times for regeneration in modern life. By analysing the Moon's archetypal symbolism and cultural history, the project aims to rediscover elements of regeneration and capture the essence of human existence. It seeks to design spaces for regeneration within the context of one's daily life and inner world, without escaping environmental influences.

Drawing inspiration from the 'Charbagh' concept of Persian gardens, which symbolised paradise, and the photograph of the Duke family on the Moon (1972), the project reinterprets the moon experience into personal spaces through the Moon Garden Rug. The Moon Garden Rug reimagines the sensory experiences of the outside world, integrating physical reality and digital virtuality into personal space.
